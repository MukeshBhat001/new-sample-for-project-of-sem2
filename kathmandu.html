Kathmandu

The Cultural Heart of Nepal

About Kathmandu

Kathmandu, the capital and largest city of Nepal, is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly blends ancient traditions with modern life. Home to over 1 million people, this historic city sits in the Kathmandu Valley at an elevation of approximately 1,400 meters above sea level.

Major Attractions

Kathmandu Durbar Square: A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient palaces, temples, and courtyards that showcase traditional Newar architecture.

Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple): An ancient Buddhist stupa perched on a hilltop, offering panoramic views of the Kathmandu Valley.

Boudhanath Stupa: One of the largest Buddhist stupas in the world and a major pilgrimage site for Tibetan Buddhists.

Pashupatinath Temple: A sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Bagmati River.

Culture & Lifestyle

Kathmandu is the cultural hub of Nepal, where you can experience traditional festivals, taste authentic Nepali cuisine, and explore bustling markets. The city is famous for its wood carvings, metalwork, and traditional crafts.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the ideal time to visit Kathmandu when the weather is pleasant and skies are clear, offering great views of the surrounding mountains.
