Chitwan National Park

Nepal's Premier Wildlife Destination

About Chitwan National Park

Chitwan National Park, established in 1973, is Nepal's first national park and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Covering 932 square kilometers in the subtropical lowlands of south-central Nepal, it's renowned for its rich biodiversity and successful conservation efforts.

Wildlife & Nature

One-Horned Rhinoceros: Home to over 600 greater one-horned rhinoceros, making it one of the most successful conservation stories in Asia.

Bengal Tigers: The park shelters around 120 Royal Bengal tigers, though they are elusive and sightings require patience and luck.

Bird Life: Over 500 bird species have been recorded, making it a paradise for birdwatchers and ornithologists.

Other Wildlife: Asian elephants, leopards, sloth bears, crocodiles, and various deer species call this park home.

Activities

Jungle Safari: Explore the park on elephant back or in jeeps to spot wildlife in their natural habitat.

Canoe Rides: Navigate the Rapti River to see crocodiles, birds, and other aquatic wildlife.

Cultural Programs: Experience traditional Tharu culture through dance performances and village visits.

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers the best wildlife viewing opportunities with clear weather and active animals. The dry season (February-April) is ideal for spotting animals near water sources.
