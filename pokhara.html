Pokhara

The City of Lakes and Adventure

About Pokhara

Pokhara, Nepal's second-largest city, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and serene atmosphere. Located at an altitude of 822 meters, this picturesque city serves as the gateway to the Annapurna region and is famous for its pristine lakes, magnificent mountain views, and adventure activities.

Major Attractions

Phewa Lake: The second-largest lake in Nepal, perfect for boating and offering spectacular reflections of the Annapurna range. The Tal Barahi Temple sits on a small island in the middle of the lake.

World Peace Pagoda: A Buddhist monument built by Japanese monks, offering panoramic views of Pokhara Valley, Phewa Lake, and the Himalayan range.

Davis Falls: A unique waterfall that flows directly into a narrow and deep canal with no end visible. Also known as Patale Chhango in Nepali.

Sarangkot: A popular viewpoint for sunrise and sunset views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges.

Adventure Activities

Pokhara is the adventure capital of Nepal, offering paragliding, ultralight flights, zip-lining, bungee jumping, and white-water rafting. It's also the starting point for many famous treks including the Annapurna Circuit and Annapurna Base Camp.

Best Time to Visit

September to November and March to May are the best times to visit Pokhara for clear mountain views and pleasant weather. The monsoon season (June-August) brings heavy rainfall but also lush green landscapes.
