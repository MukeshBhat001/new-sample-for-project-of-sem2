Langtang Valley Trek

The Valley of Glaciers

About Langtang Valley Trek

The Langtang Valley Trek, often called the "Valley of Glaciers," is one of Nepal's most accessible and rewarding treks. Located north of Kathmandu, this beautiful valley offers stunning mountain views, rich Tamang culture, and diverse flora and fauna, all while being less crowded than other popular trekking routes.

Trek Highlights

Kyanjin Gompa (3,870m): An ancient Buddhist monastery with spectacular views of Langtang Lirung and surrounding peaks.

Langtang Village: Experience authentic Tamang culture and traditional stone houses (rebuilt after the 2015 earthquake).

Rhododendron Forests: Trek through beautiful forests of rhododendron, oak, and pine, especially spectacular during spring blooming season.

Tserko Ri (4,984m): Optional climb for panoramic views of the Langtang range and Tibetan peaks.

Trek Details

Duration: 7-10 days round trip from Kathmandu

Difficulty: Moderate - suitable for beginners with good fitness

Maximum Altitude: 4,984m at Tserko Ri (optional)

Best Seasons: March-May and September-November

Cultural Experience

The Langtang region is home to the Tamang people, whose culture has been influenced by Tibetan traditions. Visit traditional villages, ancient monasteries, and experience warm hospitality while learning about Buddhist customs and mountain life.

Wildlife & Nature

Langtang National Park protects diverse wildlife including red pandas, Himalayan black bears, and over 250 bird species. The valley is also famous for its yak herds and beautiful alpine meadows.

Why Choose Langtang?

Perfect for those seeking a shorter trek without compromising on mountain views and cultural experiences. Less crowded than Everest and Annapurna regions, offering a more intimate mountain experience. Easy access from Kathmandu makes it ideal for limited time schedules.
