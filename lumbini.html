Lumbini

The Sacred Birthplace of Lord Buddha

About Lumbini

Lumbini, located in the Rupandehi District of Nepal, is one of the most sacred places for Buddhists worldwide. It is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became known as Buddha, the founder of Buddhism. This UNESCO World Heritage Site attracts millions of pilgrims and visitors from around the globe.

Sacred Sites

Maya Devi Temple: The main temple marking the exact spot where Queen Maya Devi gave birth to Prince Siddhartha in 623 BC. The temple houses the famous Marker Stone and ancient sculptures.

Sacred Pond: The holy pond where Queen Maya Devi is believed to have taken a ritual bath before giving birth to Buddha. The pond is also where the newborn prince received his first bath.

Ashoka Pillar: Erected by Emperor Ashoka in 249 BC, this pillar bears an inscription in Brahmi script that identifies Lumbini as Buddha's birthplace.

Monastic Zone: The area is divided into Eastern and Western Monastic Zones, where various countries have built monasteries representing different Buddhist traditions and architectural styles.

International Monasteries

Lumbini features beautiful monasteries built by different countries including Myanmar, Thailand, Sri Lanka, Japan, China, Germany, and many others. Each monastery showcases unique architectural styles and Buddhist traditions from their respective countries.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the ideal time to visit Lumbini when the weather is pleasant and comfortable for walking around the extensive site. The Buddha Jayanti festival in May is also a special time to experience the spiritual atmosphere.
