Annapurna Circuit Trek

Nepal's Classic Himalayan Journey

About Annapurna Circuit Trek

The Annapurna Circuit is considered one of the world's classic long-distance treks, offering incredible diversity in landscapes, cultures, and experiences. This epic journey takes you around the entire Annapurna massif, showcasing everything from subtropical forests to high-altitude desert.

Trek Highlights

Thorong La Pass (5,416m): The highest point of the trek offering spectacular 360-degree mountain views and a true sense of achievement.

Diverse Landscapes: Experience dramatic changes from lush rhododendron forests to arid Tibetan-like plateau landscapes.

Cultural Diversity: Encounter Hindu, Buddhist, and Bon cultures as you traverse different ethnic regions.

Annapurna Sanctuary: Optional side trip to Annapurna Base Camp for close-up views of Annapurna I.

Trek Details

Duration: 15-20 days depending on route and side trips

Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging - good fitness required

Maximum Altitude: 5,416m at Thorong La Pass

Best Seasons: March-May and September-November

Route Variations

The classic circuit can be done clockwise or counter-clockwise. Many trekkers now start from Besisahar and end in Pokhara, though road construction has changed some traditional routes. The trek can be customized with various entry and exit points.

Cultural Experience

Visit ancient monasteries, experience traditional Gurung and Thakali hospitality, and witness the transition from Hindu culture in the south to Tibetan Buddhist culture in the north. The trek offers insights into traditional mountain life and customs.
