Everest Base Camp Trek

The Ultimate Himalayan Adventure

About Everest Base Camp Trek

The Everest Base Camp Trek is the world's most famous trekking route, taking adventurers to the foot of the world's highest mountain. This iconic journey offers breathtaking mountain views, rich Sherpa culture, and the ultimate sense of achievement.

Trek Highlights

Everest Base Camp (5,364m): Stand at the base of Mount Everest and witness the incredible Khumbu Icefall where climbers begin their summit attempts.

Kala Patthar (5,545m): The highest point of the trek offering the best views of Mount Everest, especially at sunrise.

Namche Bazaar: The bustling Sherpa capital and acclimatization stop with markets, cafes, and stunning mountain views.

Tengboche Monastery: A spiritual highlight featuring an ancient Buddhist monastery with panoramic Himalayan views.

Trek Details

Duration: 12-14 days round trip from Lukla

Difficulty: Challenging - requires good physical fitness and mental preparation

Maximum Altitude: 5,545m at Kala Patthar

Best Seasons: March-May and September-November

What to Expect

Experience Sherpa hospitality in traditional tea houses, cross suspension bridges over deep gorges, visit ancient monasteries, and witness some of the world's most spectacular mountain scenery including Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, and Ama Dablam.

Preparation Tips

Physical fitness is crucial - start training months in advance. Proper gear including warm clothing, good boots, and altitude sickness medication are essential. Consider hiring a local guide and porter to support the local economy.
